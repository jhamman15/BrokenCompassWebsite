Check out some different Gear to make your adventure go a little smoother!

Although certain things are definitely not necessary, it is beneficial to have some good gear with you for your trip.

There are many different types of boots for all different terrain. Below are different things to take into account when selecting a pair of boots.

Height - How high a shoe come up on your ankle is very important for terrain and warmth.

Short Height - These boots are best for light warm weather days. They do not provide much ankle support but allow for a fast, quick hike.

Mid Height - These are a good catch all boot. If you are unsure which to go with, these will most likely fit any terrain you will come across. A mid height boot provide good ankle support for rough or slippery terrain.

Tall Height - These boots are generally best for cold-weather hikes. If you plan to do lots of camping or hiking during winter months, or you live in an area that gets lots of snow, this is a good option. This type of boot supports your ankle extremely well and provide great warmth for your toes.

If you are spending the night outdoors somewhere a tent does come in rather handy to keep the water off and the rodents at bay.

There are some great options for tents and even some hammock options if that better suites you style. We go through some tents below to try and help you find one that meets your needs.

The most obvious question is how many people will you be travelling with, and what season? Another thing to think about is gear storage? Some of the tents below come in multiple sizes and some have vestibule's for you to store your gear and keep it mostly dry.

A good sleeping bag is nice to have on those cold, cool nights. Not to mention the extra cusion to ease the the pain of those roots and rocks sticking into your back.

There are a few important things about picking out a sleeping bag. The biggest one is the temperature rating. Knowing roughly the array of temperature you plan to be out in will make it much easier to choose. If cold-weather camping, it's almost always better to be go with a bit warmer instead of the opposite.
